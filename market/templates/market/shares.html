What are share CFDs?

A CFD (Contract for Difference) is a financial instrument that is used to buy shares online. You can speculate on the prices of popular company shares with CFDs without actually owning the shares.

Share CFDs allow you to go both long (buy) or short (sell) to speculate on a price rise or fall respectively.

What moves the price of shares?

Earnings reports

Earnings reports released every quarter and once a year can influence a company’s share price as traders and investors use revenue, profit, and earnings per share (EPS) figures as part of their fundamental analysis.

Macroeconomic data

Data releases such as gross domestic product (GDP) and retail sales can significantly influence company share prices. Strong data can make prices rise, while weak data can make them fall.

Market sentiment

Market sentiment refers to the psychology of market participants on a particular stock which can cause demand to fluctuate.

Interest rates

When interest rates are low, the stock market might have greater activity because more people may invest in stocks and shares to achieve greater returns than saving their money in a bank account.
